# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

European tourists planning holidays on the Costa Brava. They arrive from diverse countries (Spain, UK, France, Germany, Netherlands, Italy, Catalonia, Russia) and browse primarily on mobile, often outdoors in bright sunlight.

Two modes coexist: **explorers** comparing boat rental options across providers, and **decided visitors** ready to book. The interface must serve both — inspire the undecided while making action effortless for the ready.

The job to be done: turn a few free hours on the coast into an unforgettable memory on the water, without friction, without doubt, and without needing to be an expert.

## Product Purpose

Public-facing booking request platform for a boat rental business in Blanes, Costa Brava. The website is the storefront — it must sell the experience before any conversation happens. Success is a completed booking request from a visitor who felt confident enough to commit without speaking to anyone first.

No online payments. The web captures intent; the business handles the rest manually via WhatsApp and phone.

## Brand Personality

**Libertad, seguridad, memoria.**

- **Libertad**: the open sea, no itinerary, your own pace. The interface should feel spacious and unhurried.
- **Seguridad**: trustworthy, transparent, no surprises. Clear pricing, honest information, professional presentation.
- **Memoria**: this is about creating moments that last. The design should feel warm and personal, not transactional.

Voice: confident but never corporate. Local but never amateur. Like a friend who knows the coast and wants to share it with you.

## Anti-references

- **Luxury yacht charters** — too exclusive, too cold, too aspirational. This is accessible boating, not superyacht marketing.
- **Budget excursion sites** — clip art, stock photos, crowded layouts, aggressive CTAs. No cheap tourism aesthetics.
- **Generic travel agencies** — Booking.com-style UI, cookie-cutter templates, soulless conversion machines. This should feel like a place, not a platform.

## Design Principles

1. **The sea sells itself** — let photography and space do the heavy lifting. The interface recedes; the experience advances.
2. **Confidence through clarity** — every price, every detail, every step of booking should feel transparent and straightforward. No hidden information, no ambiguity.
3. **Mobile under the sun** — most visitors browse on phones, often outdoors. High contrast, generous touch targets, fast loads, legible in direct sunlight.
4. **Warmth over polish** — prefer a Mediterranean warmth that feels human and inviting over cold, hyper-polished tech aesthetics. Professional, but never sterile.
5. **One conversation away** — the site captures a request, not a payment. The tone should feel like the beginning of a conversation, not the end of a transaction.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA compliance (already in place).
- Mobile-first design — majority of traffic is mobile.
- High contrast ratios critical — outdoor/sunlight usage is common.
- Touch targets minimum 44px (already enforced).
- 8-language support (es, en, ca, fr, de, nl, it, ru) — text must accommodate variable string lengths.
- Safe areas for iOS notch/home bar (already handled).
