# Boat Trip from Blanes 2026: the complete guide to Costa Brava on your own terms

Planning a boat trip from Blanes in 2026? This guide saves you hours of googling. You'll find the route options that actually make sense, 2026 prices in hard numbers, what comes included and how to book without surprises. Written by the team that casts off from Blanes harbor every single day.

Blanes is the southernmost port of the Costa Brava and the fastest starting point if you're coming from Barcelona or the Maresme coast. Within 30 minutes of sailing you reach coves that are only accessible by sea, with crystal-clear water and a fraction of the crowds you'll find at the main beaches.

## Why Blanes is the smart starting port

Blanes harbor is small, has easy parking and gives you direct access to a spectacular stretch of coast without hours of sailing. Compared to larger ports like Port Olimpic in Barcelona, you're anchored over turquoise water in 10-15 minutes.

Three practical reasons to depart from Blanes:

- **Short hop to the coves**: in 20 minutes you're at Cala Sant Francesc, one of the best swim-and-snorkel spots on the southern Costa Brava.
- **Easy to reach**: 36 minutes by train from Barcelona-Passeig de Gracia (R1 line), free parking near the harbor and fuel stations nearby.
- **Milder average wind**: the coast from Blanes to Tossa is more sheltered from the garbi (southwesterly) than other spots, which you'll notice on summer afternoons.

## Types of boat trip from Blanes

Not every trip is the same. Based on your experience, budget and how hands-on you want to be, there are three clear options.

### Boat trip without a skipper license

This is the most popular choice and the one we recommend to most families and groups of friends. With our [boats without license](/en/boats-without-license) (up to 15 HP) you skipper yourself without any license, as long as you're over 18 and sign the standard conditions.

- Capacity: 4-5 people.
- Reasonable range: up to Lloret on 2-3h trips, up to Santa Cristina on a 4h trip.
- **Fuel included** on every license-free boat. No surprises at the end.
- Great for first-timers: we walk you through everything before you cast off.

### Boat trip with a skipper license

If you hold a PER or equivalent nautical license, our [boats with license](/en/boats-with-license) open up the whole coast. These are bigger engines (up to 140 HP), fit 6-7 people and can reach Sant Feliu de Guixols or even Palamos on full-day charters.

- Capacity: 6-7 people.
- More speed and stability in open sea.
- **Fuel NOT included**: you only pay for what you burn (great on shorter routes).
- Requires PER license or equivalent and a minimum of experience.

### Private skippered trip

If you'd rather not steer, the private skippered trip is the premium option: you pick the itinerary, we handle the boat. Ideal with small kids, when you want to fully relax, or for half-day and full-day charters. ## Recommended routes: what to see on your boat trip

These are the three routes that work best depending on the duration you book, ordered from shortest to longest so you pick what fits your day.

### Short route (2h): Cala Sant Francesc and Cala Treumal

Perfect for a first taste of the sea, families with small kids or couples looking for a mid-morning escape. You leave Blanes harbor, round Palomera island on your port side and in 15 minutes you're anchored in **Cala Sant Francesc**, the first cove north of Blanes, sheltered and with a rocky bottom that's great for snorkeling. Five minutes further north is **Cala Treumal**, smaller and quieter.

### Medium route (4h): Lloret, Cala Boadella and Santa Cristina

The most balanced option. You push north to **Lloret beach**, detour into **Cala Boadella** (semi-nudist, small caves) and finish anchored at **Santa Cristina**, the most photographed cove around Lloret, with its hilltop chapel overlooking the sea. If Lloret is closer to your hotel, our [Lloret de Mar boat rental](/en/boat-rental-lloret-de-mar) base is an option, but from Blanes the route is just as quick.

### Long route (6-8h): up to Tossa de Mar and Cala Giverola

The full-day gem. You sail the entire sheltered coast up to **Tossa de Mar** (with its medieval castle visible from the sea), pass Cala Pola and stop at **Cala Giverola**, one of the best snorkeling coves on the whole Costa Brava. Round trip with comfortable swim stops, lunch on board and a nap at anchor. This route is only recommended with a licensed boat or a professional skipper, due to range and open-sea conditions.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need experience to drive a license-free boat from Blanes?**
No. You only need to be over 18. Before casting off we run a 20-30 minute hands-on briefing covering engine, GPS, anchoring, communications and basic rules of the road.

**Can I book on the same day?**
It depends on the season. In low season (April, May, June, September, October) there are usually slots. In July and August we recommend booking 5-10 days ahead, especially for 6-8h durations.

**What if the weather is bad?**
If the port authority issues a warning or conditions aren't safe (wind above force 4, choppy sea), we reschedule the booking at no cost or refund it in full. Safety first.

**Is it suitable for kids?**
Yes. License-free boats are perfect for families. We provide child-sized life jackets. For babies and very young kids we recommend short routes close to shore.

**How many people fit on a license-free boat?**
Depends on the model: 4 people on the Astec 400, 5 people on Solar 450, Remus 450 and Astec 480. For groups of 6-7 you need a licensed boat.

**Can I fish during the trip?**
Yes, but you need the Generalitat recreational fishing license. It's a quick online procedure (about 10 EUR per year).
