# What running a boat rental on the Costa Brava taught me about seasonal pricing — and why we publish the numbers

**Hashtags:** #CostaBrava #TourismBusiness #SmallBusiness #Spain #TravelIndustry #PricingStrategy #LocalTourism #Mediterranean

---

Most of my peers in the Spanish coastal tourism sector don't publish real prices online. You'll find a contact form, a brochure, a vague "from XX€," and the actual quote arrives by email 24 hours later — often adjusted to what the customer looks like they can afford.

When we started Costa Brava Rent a Boat in Puerto de Blanes, we made a different call: publish every price, every season, every boat, on the public website. Eight years later, here's what I've learned from running a boat rental operation this way — and why I think it matters for any small business operating in a tourist-heavy market.

**1. Transparent pricing disqualifies the wrong customers faster**

A phone call with someone who was expecting 60€/hour when our reality is 115€ for two hours is a lost hour for both of us. When prices are on the site, the conversation that does happen is about the boat, the route, and what's included — not about whether we can negotiate.

**2. Season-based pricing is not gouging if it's disclosed**

We run three price tiers: low (April-June, September-October), mid (July), high (August). In August, demand outstrips supply by roughly 4:1 on our Solar 450 fleet. Higher August prices are how we make April viable, when boats sit idle for three or four days a week. The model works because the customer sees both ends of the curve on the same page.

Published 2026 numbers for our most-booked boat:
- 2h: 115€ (low) / 130€ (mid) / 140€ (high)
- 4h: 150€ / 180€ / 195€
- 6h: 190€ / 230€ / 240€
- 8h: 220€ / 270€ / 290€

**3. Fuel included is a feature, not a line item to hide**

Our unlicensed boats (up to 15HP, no license needed) include fuel. Our licensed boats don't. Both options are disclosed. We lose the occasional budget-conscious customer who would have been happier with "fuel extra" — but we save every "why is my final bill 40€ higher than what we agreed" conversation.

**4. Geography is undervalued**

Blanes is not the most famous port on the Costa Brava. But it's 36 minutes by commuter train from Barcelona, 15 minutes of navigation from the first swim-worthy cove, and genuinely sheltered from the summer southwesterly. Our marketing for years has been pointing out a geographic advantage that most competitors north of us don't have.

**5. A 4.8/5 rating across 307 reviews compounds more than any ad campaign**

Spend on ads gets us bookings this month. Operational quality and transparent pricing gets us the next five years of bookings, because the reviews do the selling.

---

For anyone running a small tourism operation on the Mediterranean, the temptation to be "flexible" on pricing is real. What I'd offer, after 8 seasons: publish the numbers, stand behind them, and let the honest pricing be part of the product.
