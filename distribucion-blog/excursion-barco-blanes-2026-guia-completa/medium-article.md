# Why Blanes Is the Smart Starting Point for a Costa Brava Boat Trip in 2026

*A boat captain's perspective on the quieter, cheaper alternative to launching from Barcelona.*

**Tags:** Costa Brava, Boat Rental, Spain Travel, Mediterranean, Day Trip, Barcelona, Sailing, Travel Tips

---

Most visitors planning a day on the water near Barcelona end up Googling "boat rental Port Olímpic" and paying a premium for the privilege of spending their first 45 minutes of navigation staring at cargo ships. There is a quieter option, 60 kilometers up the coast, that regulars have been using for years: Blanes.

I run boats out of Blanes harbor almost every day from April through October. In 2026 we're seeing more international visitors discover the same thing locals have known for a while — Blanes is the southernmost gateway to the Costa Brava, and it shortens the distance to the calas that actually make the region famous.

This piece isn't a brochure. It's a practical breakdown of what a boat day from Blanes looks like in 2026: what you'll pay, where you'll go, what's actually included, and the decision between a licensed and unlicensed boat.

## The Geography Argument

Blanes sits right at the southern tip of the Costa Brava. Northbound, within 10 to 15 minutes of leaving the harbor, you're dropping anchor in turquoise water at Cala Sant Francesc. Compare that with the Port Olímpic in Barcelona: you'll spend the better part of an hour motoring out before you see anything resembling a cove.

The other practical argument is the approach. Blanes is 36 minutes on the R1 commuter train from Passeig de Gràcia. Free parking at the port. Gas stations nearby. Nothing exotic — just logistics that work.

Wind-wise, the stretch from Blanes to Tossa de Mar sits slightly sheltered from the *garbí* (the southwesterly that typically picks up in summer afternoons). You feel it less here than you do further south toward Barcelona.

## Three Ways to Do a Boat Day

There are three categories worth understanding before you book anything.

**Unlicensed boats (up to 15 HP)** — the category most families and groups of friends end up in. You don't need a nautical license, just to be 18 or older. You drive yourself after a 20-30 minute briefing. Capacity 4-5 people. Fuel is included in the price, which sounds small until you compare it to operators who add gasoline at the end and surprise you with a 40€ bill. Range is realistic: Lloret in 2-3 hours, Santa Cristina in 4.

**Licensed boats (up to 140 HP)** — if you hold a PER or equivalent, this opens up the whole coast. Capacity 6-7, enough speed to reach Sant Feliu de Guíxols or Palamós in a full day. Important caveat: fuel is *not* included on licensed boats. You pay what you burn, which rewards efficient routing.

**Private charter with skipper** — if you'd rather not drive. Someone experienced handles the boat, you pick the itinerary. Makes sense with very young kids, for a pure-relaxation day, or for groups that want something close to a private yacht experience without the yacht price.

## What a Half-Day Actually Costs in 2026

Here are the real numbers for our Solar 450 (5-person, unlicensed, fuel included):

- Low season (April-June, September-October): 150€ for 4 hours, 190€ for 6 hours
- Mid season (July): 180€ for 4 hours, 230€ for 6 hours
- High season (August): 195€ for 4 hours, 240€ for 6 hours

Split between four or five people, the math lands somewhere between a nice dinner and a modest excursion ticket. Licensed boats start around 280€ for 4 hours in low season, fuel separate.

There's also a refundable deposit (200-500€ depending on the boat) held on your card the day of. You get it all back if the boat comes back in the same condition.

## Routes Worth the Fuel

**Short loop (2 hours).** Out of the harbor, around Isla Palomera, drop anchor in Cala Sant Francesc. Rocky bottom, excellent snorkeling, protected from wind. Five minutes further: Cala Treumal, smaller, less crowded. This is the route I recommend to first-timers and families with small kids.

**Mid loop (4 hours).** Continue past Sant Francesc toward Lloret de Mar, detour to Cala Boadella (small caves, clothing-optional beach, fewer crowds than you'd expect), anchor at Santa Cristina — the cove with the hermitage that's on every Costa Brava postcard.

**Full day (6-8 hours).** All the way to Tossa de Mar with the medieval castle visible from the water, past Cala Pola, ending at Cala Giverola — one of the best snorkeling spots in the entire Costa Brava. Only sensible on a licensed boat or with a skipper, because you're in more exposed water for part of the route.

## The Cost Calculus vs. a Tourist Excursion

A typical "boat excursion ticket" from Lloret or Blanes runs 20-30€ per person for a 90-minute ride on a crowded catamaran. Four adults = 80-120€. For 150€ you can rent a Solar 450, go wherever you want, anchor wherever you like, and stop for a swim whenever you feel like it. The ticketed excursion is cheaper per hour on paper, but you're paying for a queue, a schedule, and a view from somebody else's boat.

The rental model also lets you leave your stuff on board, bring your own food and drink, and pick your own music.

## The Things Nobody Mentions in the Brochure

- Reserve 5-10 days ahead for July and August, especially for 6+ hour rentals. In April, May, September and October, same-day reservations are often possible.
- Bad weather: if the port authority issues an advisory or conditions aren't safe, you get a free date change or a refund. It's in every operator's contract and it gets used every summer.
- Fishing is legal but you need a Generalitat de Catalunya recreational fishing permit — an online process, about 10€ a year.
- Most boats have USB or Bluetooth for music. Bring a phone and a cable.
- A GoPro or waterproof camera is worth more than another towel. The calas reward it.
