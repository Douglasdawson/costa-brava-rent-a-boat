## Post 1 — Costa Brava / Blanes Forum

**Thread to create or reply in:** "Renting a boat from Blanes — tips for 2026?" or any existing "boat day" thread

**Suggested title (if starting new thread):** Boat rental from Blanes 2026 — what actually works, from multiple trips

**Post:**

We've been doing a boat day on the Costa Brava for a few years now and wanted to share what we've figured out for 2026, in case it helps anyone in the planning stage.

**Why Blanes specifically:**

Most travelers default to Lloret or to renting from Barcelona directly. Both work, but Blanes has two practical advantages we didn't appreciate until our second trip: (1) it's the southernmost port of the Costa Brava, so within 15 minutes of leaving the harbor you're anchored in your first cala — no long stretch through industrial water like you get out of Port Olímpic; (2) it's 36 minutes by commuter train from Barcelona (R1 line, Passeig de Gràcia station, trains every 30 min), so it's genuinely day-trippable without a rental car.

**License or no license:**

If your group is 4-5 people, the "sin licencia" (unlicensed) category is perfect. Spanish law lets anyone 18+ drive a boat up to 15HP without any certification. You get a 20-30 min briefing at the port and off you go. Fuel is included on these boats, which I didn't realize the first time — I'd budgeted an extra 40€ that never materialized.

If you're 6-7 people or want to go further than Tossa, you need a licensed boat (PER or equivalent) or to hire a skipper.

**Our typical route:**

We do a 4-hour rental in the morning. Out of the port, Cala Sant Francesc for the first swim (15 min from harbor, excellent snorkeling), continue to Cala Treumal, then up to Santa Cristina for a longer anchor and lunch on board. Back to port before the wind picks up (the southwesterly typically arrives mid-afternoon in summer).

**What we bring:**

Food and drinks in a cooler (operators rent coolers for ~5€ if you don't have one), snorkel masks (rentals are 7-8€ but ours are better), reef-safe sunscreen, a waterproof phone case, cash for the deposit release, and a Bluetooth speaker — most boats have USB/Bluetooth but it's hit or miss.

**Pricing for 2026:**

Low season (April-June, Sept-Oct): roughly 150€ for a 4-hour rental of a 5-person boat, fuel included.
High season (August): around 195€ for the same.
Licensed boats start at roughly 280€ for 4h, fuel extra.

Split across 4-5 people, it's usually cheaper per person than the ticketed group excursions, and dramatically better.

**Logistics:**

Book 5-10 days ahead for July/August, especially for 6-hour rentals. Shoulder season is often same-day possible.

Bad-weather policy: the operators we've used rebooked us free of charge when conditions weren't safe. Ask specifically about this before paying.

Fishing is legal but needs a Generalitat de Catalunya recreational permit (online, ~10€/year).

## Post 2 — Barcelona Forum (adapted angle, different framing)

**Thread to reply in:** any thread asking "day trips from Barcelona" or "what to do from BCN by train"

**Post:**

For a one-day trip out of Barcelona that doesn't involve a bus tour, renting a small boat from Blanes is worth considering. Fully doable without a rental car and less chaotic than you'd expect.

**Time plan from Barcelona:**

- 08:30 train from Passeig de Gràcia, R1 line to Blanes (36 min, ~4€)
- 09:15 walk to the port (10 min)
- 10:00 boat briefing and departure
- Boat day — typical 4-hour rental puts you back around 14:30
- Lunch in Blanes or back on the train
- 18:00-19:00 back in Barcelona

**Which boat:**

Unlicensed boats up to 15HP are legal to drive without any nautical certification if you're 18+. They take 4-5 people, you get a 20-30 minute briefing at the port, and fuel is included in the quoted price. If you have a PER or equivalent nautical license, you can rent a bigger boat (up to 140HP, 6-7 people).

**What you see from the water that you don't see by land:**

Most of the calas between Blanes and Tossa are genuinely only accessible by boat. Cala Sant Francesc has a road access but it's a 15-minute walk down; from a boat it's 15 minutes from the harbor and you drop anchor right in front. Same for Santa Cristina, Cala Boadella, Cala Giverola — all require either a long walk down a cliff path or a boat.

**Costs for 2026 (4 people):**

150€ for 4h boat rental, low season.
+ ~16€ train round trip per person (BCN to Blanes).
+ ~5€ optional cooler rental.
+ Food/drinks you bring.

Works out to around 45€ per person for a full boat day with transport. Way less than a ticketed excursion and dramatically more flexible.
