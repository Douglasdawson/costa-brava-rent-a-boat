# TripAdvisor Forum Post — Costa Brava Forum

**Topic/Thread:** "What should I bring for a boat rental day on the Costa Brava?"

**Category:** Costa Brava Forum > Things to Do

---

Hi everyone,

I see this question come up regularly so I thought I'd share some practical advice. I work at a boat rental company in Blanes (between Barcelona and the French border) and we see hundreds of customers each summer. Here's what the well-prepared ones always bring — and what the others wish they had.

**Absolute essentials:**

- **Sunscreen SPF 50+, water-resistant.** The UV reflection off the Mediterranean is intense. Apply before boarding and bring the bottle to reapply. People consistently underestimate this.
- **Water: 1.5 liters per person minimum.** Sun + wind + activity = much faster dehydration than you'd expect.
- **A dry bag or waterproof pouch** for your phone, keys, and wallet. Costs about 5 euros and prevents the most common disaster of any boat day. Trust me.
- **Shoes with non-slip, non-marking soles.** Water shoes are ideal. Regular flip-flops are slippery on wet decks, and dark-soled shoes leave permanent marks. White or clear soles only.

**Highly recommended:**

- **Snorkel gear** (at least mask and snorkel tube). The rocky seabed along the Costa Brava is genuinely beautiful. Cala Sant Francesc near Blanes is particularly impressive underwater.
- **A soft cooler** with fruit, water, and light snacks. Rigid coolers don't fit well on smaller boats.
- **A light jacket or windbreaker** for the return trip. Even in July/August, the wind chill when the boat is moving at sunset is surprisingly cool.
- **Microfiber towels** — they dry fast and take up much less space than regular towels.
- **Waterproof phone case** if you want underwater photos. The ones that cost 10-15 euros work fine.

**What to leave at the hotel:**

Hard luggage (soft bags only), valuables you won't use, black-soled shoes, and anything in aerosol cans (heat risk).

**Good news — most rental companies include:**

Life jackets, sun canopy (bimini), anchor, full fuel tank, safety kit, and often a map of the best coves. So don't worry about bringing any of that.

**Quick tip for the area:** The coves between Blanes and Tossa de Mar are stunning and many are only accessible by boat. If you only have half a day, head north toward Sa Palomera and Cala Sant Francesc — you won't regret it.

We put together a detailed packing checklist on our website if you want to save it to your phone before your trip: https://www.costabravarentaboat.com/es/blog/que-llevar-dia-barco-checklist (the page is in Spanish but the final checklist section is easy to follow regardless of language).

Enjoy your boat day!
